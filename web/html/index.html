Welcome to Market Meteor!

  • Market Meteor visualizes quantitative data for over 1500 stocks. These stocks have a long history of data and over 2 billion market cap.
  • Data is updated every night with the exception of live Watchlist, which updates on loading of this site. Users may also refresh the data by clicking Refresh or Refresh Holdings.
  • Our algorithm detects, Market Meteors (also referred to as signals), stocks that have fallen and may be primed for a short-term rebound.
  • A prediction can be made for when a Market Meteor will arrive, and is denoted as the Target Price in the watchlist. A stock must close at this target price in order to become a Market Meteor.
  • Market Meteors are evaluated over a span of 63 days.
  • Profitability may vary depending on your risk tolerance.
  • Market Meteor is for entertainment and informational purposes and does not constitute investment advice

63d% vs. Earnings Offset Closest Overview

  • This is a scatter plot of the 63d% (63 day delta) versus Earnings Offset Closest.
  • Recall that earnings offset is defined as: the number of days from an earnings report. Negative values indicate days before earnings. Positive values indicate days after earnings.
  • and chances are defined as: In a window of 63 days, looking forward, the number of days or chances a position can be closed with a gain of at least 5%
  • green dots represent signals that had 6 or more chances
  • yellow dots represent signals that had 1-5 chances
  • red dots represent signals that had 0 chances
  • dots that are outlined only and are not filled in are "in-progress" of being observed in our 63 day window

Close vs. Date Analysis Overview

  • The purple series represents the Close price
  • When hovering, the data callout gives key information:
    • 1d %: last day's percent change
    • 63d %: percent change from the 63-day peak
    • Chances: In a window of 63 days, looking forward, the number of days or chances a position can be closed with a gain of at least 5%
    • Earnings Offset Closest: Number of days from an earnings report. Negative values indicate days before earnings. Positive values indicate days after earnings.
  • Green and red dots represent the performance of the M20 algorithm. Green dots, labeled 'M20 Pass', indicate instances where the algorithm correctly forecasted a price increase of at least 5% within 63 days. Red dots, labeled 'M20 Fail', denote occurrences where the algorithm's predictions did not meet this benchmark.
  • The markers labeled 'E' denote days on which earnings were reported.

    Holdings

  • From the settings gear different portfolios can be displayed
  • Positions without a sell date are in focus
  • Positions with a sell date are dimmed in green if the Return to Date (RTD) was greater than the ^GSPC's RTD (index) over the same period, otherwise they are dimmed in grey.
  • Last price is red if negative and represents the percent change from the buy price to the last price
  • In the case of a stock split the buy prices are not adjusted and may have erroneous percent change
  • A drop down allows for selection of different portfolios

    Incoming Meteors

  • Recall that Market Meteors (also referred to as signals), are stocks that have fallen and may be primed for a short-term rebound.
  • Rows that are purple are either candidates for becoming Meteors or are already Meteors
  • If 1d % and prev 1d % are negative, that means the last two days closed with a negative percent change, and it is already a Meteor.
  • Otherwise, if 63d % is <= -20% but only 1d % is negative, it must close down for the day to become a meteor.
  • Make sure to view the other graphs to determine if meteors are right for your risk tolerance

  • Clicking the settings gear will open the filter menu
